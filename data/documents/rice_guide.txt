Rice cultivation requires careful water management.

Common problems and solutions:
- Yellowing: Iron deficiency in flooded paddies leads to chlorosis. Apply iron chelates and improve drainage.
- Brown spots: Caused by Helminthosporium oryzae (brown spot disease). Use fungicide (carbendazim) and avoid overhead irrigation.
- Blast disease: Pyricularia oryzae causes diamond-shaped lesions. Use resistant varieties and apply tricyclazole.
- Water management: Maintain 2–5 cm of standing water during vegetative stage; drain fields before harvest.
- Sustainable tip: Practice System of Rice Intensification (SRI) to reduce water use by 30–50%.
