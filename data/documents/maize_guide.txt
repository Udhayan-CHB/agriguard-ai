Maize (corn) is a staple crop grown worldwide.

Common problems and solutions:
- Yellow leaves: This is usually a sign of nitrogen deficiency. Apply nitrogen-rich fertilizer (e.g., urea) at a rate of 50–100 kg/ha. It could also indicate maize streak virus; remove infected plants and control leafhoppers.
- Leaf spots: Northern Corn Leaf Blight appears as long, grayish-green lesions. Use fungicides like mancozeb and practice crop rotation.
- Stunted growth: Often due to phosphorus deficiency. Apply phosphate fertilizers (e.g., DAP) during planting.
- Pests: Fall armyworm can devastate maize. Monitor and apply neem-based pesticides or Bt spray.
- Water management: Maize requires about 500–800 mm of water per season. Use mulch to conserve moisture.
