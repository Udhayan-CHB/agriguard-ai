Agroecological practices for sustainable farming:

- Crop rotation: Alternating crops reduces pest buildup and improves soil fertility. Rotate cereals with legumes to fix nitrogen.
- Organic composting: Use farmyard manure and crop residues to improve soil organic matter. Reduces the need for synthetic fertilizers.
- Cover cropping: Plant legumes (clover, vetch) during off-seasons to prevent erosion and fix nitrogen.
- Drip irrigation: Delivers water directly to plant roots, saving 30–60% water compared to flood irrigation.
- Agroforestry: Integrate trees (e.g., Leucaena, Grevillea) along field boundaries to provide shade, windbreak, and additional income.
- Integrated Pest Management (IPM): Use natural predators (ladybugs, lacewings) and biopesticides before resorting to chemical pesticides.
- Conservation tillage: Minimum or zero tillage reduces soil disturbance, conserves moisture, and sequesters carbon.
